From the Dolphin Whisperer

Meet Chopper — The Lone Wolf of Ole River

Week of March 30, 2026

Chopper is one of our older resident males — a full-grown bottlenose dolphin I've known for years. He got his name the hard way: an incident years ago left part of his dorsal fin missing. Not the prettiest story, but it makes him one of the easiest dolphins to spot out on the water.

Unlike the mothers and calves we see daily, Chopper is a loner. That's typical for male bottlenose dolphins — they're the hunters. Males forage on their own, and when they find food, they send out a signal through echolocation to let nearby dolphins know there's a good source close by. Before long, his friends show up to join him.

We usually spot Chopper in the mornings when he's cruising for brunch near the entrance to Ole River, or in the evenings when he's packing in his last calories before nightfall. Dolphins swim all night long but don't eat while they sleep — so that evening meal matters.

We don't see him often. Maybe once a month. But when Chopper shows up, the whole boat knows it.

— Tabby

Meet Your Crew

Tabby the Dolphin Whisperer & Captain Bart

Woman-owned. Family-run. Built from the water up.

Tabby is an Alabama girl who traded the classroom for the coastline. After years as a K–8 teacher, she followed her heart to the Gulf Coast, built Dolphin Tales with her husband Captain Bart, and never looked back. Over the past decade, she's learned every dolphin by name, every sandbar by feel, and every stretch of hidden waterfront between Orange Beach, Gulf Shores, and Perdido Key.

It hasn't been easy. Hurricanes have hit hard — their home was destroyed and they're still rebuilding. But Tabby and Bart keep showing up, keep running the boat, and keep giving families the best day of their vacation. That's what small business looks like on the Gulf Coast.

When Tabby isn't on the water, she's running Beach Bucket List — a local discount passport she created to help visitors support small businesses across the beach community. Former teacher, business owner, dolphin expert, hurricane survivor. She does it all.

Captain Bart keeps the boat running and the waters safe. Tabby keeps every guest entertained, educated, and smiling. Together, they've built something worth supporting.
